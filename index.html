Visualizing the Chinese Language



Due to it's use of logographic characters, Chinese is arguably the most complex and fascinating written language in the world. While most other civilizations and cultures developed alphabet-based writing systems, the unique and ancient Chinese writing system has survived thousands of years.

Each word in written Chinese is composed of a distinct character or combination of characters; characters offer no indication of how to pronounce the word. The pronunciations of each word must be memorized. The system is so inherently difficult that illiteracy was high in China for thousands of years. In fact, in the 1930's and 40's, Chinese scholars created simplified characters in order to improve literacy.







The construction of Chinese character may seem arbitrary to a non-speaker, however each Chinese character has deep meaning and thousands of years of history encoded in it. Each Chinese character contains a component called a "radical," which itself is a character as well. Most radicals are each a component of multiple different characters, and sometimes the meaning of the radical carries over to several of the characters it appears in. Therefore, characters that look similar often have similar or related meanings.
